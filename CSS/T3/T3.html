The Irreplaceable Value of Human Decision-Making in the Age of AI

AI's rapid advancement has ignited enthusiasm about its potential to revolutionize corporate decision-making by substituting for expensive, fallible humans. But it's naive to believe that by gathering ever more data and feeding it to ever more powerful algorithms alone, businesses can uncover the truth, make the right decisions, and create value. We call this false belief dataism.

Decisions are not merely exercises in data aggregation and algorithmic analysis. They necessarily involve many additional nuanced elements, such as selecting trustworthy data sources, employing imagination to envision possibilities beyond available facts, and judging the feasibility of solutions.
